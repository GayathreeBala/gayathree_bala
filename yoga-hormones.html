Yoga for Hormonal Balance



Yoga is more than just an enjoyable physical activity; it can also have a positive effect on your hormones. When you do yoga regularly, your body releases hormones that can help reduce stress, boost energy levels, and increase your overall sense of well-being. Let’s take a look at some of the hormones released during yoga and how they can benefit you.

The below asanas are effective in balancing hormones:



Bhujangasana


  • Start by lying on the yoga mat with your face to the floor.
  • Align your hands with your ribs and place them under your shoulders.
  • Place your feet together and point them outwards.
  • Slowly lift your chest and torso up and look up.
  • Hold the position for a few seconds and breathe.
  • Come back down and relax your legs and arms.


    • Setubandhasan

  • Start by lying down on your back.
  • Bend your knees and place your feet apart.
  • Place your hands on the floor pointing toward your feet.
  • Inhale deeply as you raise your hips above the ground to make an arch.
  • Relax your muscles and stay in position for a few seconds.
  • Bring your knees down and repeat the pose.


    • Shalabhasana

  • Start with your belly on the mat.
  • Extend your arms backward with your palms facing the floor.
  • Point your toes outwards and stretch.
  • Now raise your head and chest along with your thighs and legs.
  • Lift your hands from the floor and stretch.
  • Hold in position and breathe.
  • Come back to the original position and repeat.


    • Malasana

  • Start by standing on the mat with your feet wide apart.
  • Now start to lower down till you're in a full squat position.
  • Tuck your elbows in your inner knees.
  • Bring your palms together in a Namaskar.
  • Breathe as you stay in position for a few seconds.


    • Prasarita Padottanasana

  • Start by standing on your mat with your feet wide apart.
  • Curl your toes a little inside for better grip.
  • Inhale deeply before bending down.
  • Exhale as you bend forward. Folding your body as you go towards the ground.
  • Place your hands on the mat.
  • Breathe and hold the position.

    • Ardha Chandrasana

  • Turn the right foot towards the right and point the toes outwards.
  • Face towards your right.
  • Lift your hands up and spread them wide. One pointing forward towards the right, parallel to the extended toe, and the other back towards the left.
  • Slowly bend down from your side till you touch your ankle in your right hand.
  • Adjust the position of your legs according to your comfort.
  • Once you have adjusted your stance, lift your left hand up and point it upwards.
  • Look towards the hand lifted upwards and breathe.


    • Other Tips to Naturally Control Hormonal Imbalance


  • Get enough protein. Proteins provide amino acids that your body cannot make on its own and are needed to produce peptide hormones. These hormones play a crucial role in regulating several physiological processes, including growth, energy metabolism, appetite, stress, and more.
  • Exercise regularly. Getting enough physical activity strongly influences your hormonal health. It enhances hormone receptor sensitivity, helping the delivery of nutrients and hormone signals.
  • Maintain a moderate weight. Weight gain is directly associated with hormonal imbalances. Obesity relates to a lack of ovulation in women. Eating within your calorie range can help maintain hormonal balance.
  • Watch your gut health. Your gut produces numerous metabolites that can affect hormone health.
  • Lower sugar intake. Minimizing added sugar intake can help balance hormones. Eating added sugar promotes insulin resistance, and fructose intake is linked to disruptions in the gut microbiome, ultimately leading to hormonal imbalances.
  • Reduce stress. Stress can significantly harm the body’s hormones in many ways. Work to decrease and manage your daily stress.
  • Get enough sleep. Sleep is one of the most important factors in hormonal imbalance. The levels of hormones can rise and fall throughout the day due to the quality of sleep.
  • Eat healthy fats. Healthy fat intake can work to maintain balanced hormones that are involved with appetite, metabolism, and feeling full.
  • Eat plenty of fiber. Fiber plays a role in gut health, helping regulate hormones like insulin.

    • Other Problems Caused by Hormonal Imbalance

  • Weight gain/loss
  • Muscle weakness,aches, tenderness or stiffness
  • Increased thirst or sweating
  • Mood Swings
  • Puffy Face
